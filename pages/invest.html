Executive Summary

ClimateCurtainsAB presents a compelling investment opportunity to capture a significant share of the multi-billion dollar industrial energy efficiency market. We have developed and commercialized a patented, high-performance thermal curtain system (Swedish patent SE540848C2) that directly targets one of the largest sources of industrial energy loss: windows. Our solution offers a non-invasive, cost-effective, and high-ROI alternative to traditional retrofitting, with a proven 18-month average payback period.

The demand for our technology is driven by a powerful combination of escalating energy costs and stringent government decarbonization mandates across Europe and Asia. The Asia-Pacific industrial energy efficiency market alone is projected to reach USD 70.58 billion by 2030. With a proven product validated by the prestigious Energy Globe Award and successful deployment in high-profile sustainability projects, ClimateCurtainsAB is at a key inflection point.

We are seeking **USD 5-8 million in Series A funding** to execute a detailed, phased expansion strategy: deepen our penetration into key European industrial markets and launch a partnership-driven entry into Asia, beginning with China, followed by Japan and South Korea. This investment will scale our proven technology to meet clear and accelerating global demand, delivering significant returns to our partners and a measurable impact on global energy consumption.

I. The Market Opportunity: A Multi-Billion Dollar Mandate for Efficiency

The global Industrial Energy Efficiency Market is valued at USD 27.28 billion in 2025 and is projected to expand to USD 48.6 billion by 2032. This growth is propelled by a dual-engine of regulatory mandates and stark economic realities, de-risking the market and ensuring sustained, non-discretionary demand.

Powerful Regulatory and Economic Drivers

Europe: The market is mature and regulation-driven. The European Union has a legally binding target to improve energy efficiency by at least 32.5% by 2030 and has mandated a transition to a zero-emission building stock by 2050, compelling a vast industrial base to invest in compliant technologies like ClimateCurtains.

Asia: Growth is even more pronounced, driven by forceful government action.

  • China: As the primary engine of growth, the government's 2024-2025 Energy Conservation and Carbon Reduction Action Plan mandates a 2.5% improvement in national energy intensity, with a more aggressive goal of 3.5% for large-scale industries. This creates immediate, large-scale demand across China's enormous industrial sector.
  • Japan: Aiming for a 35% improvement in energy consumption efficiency by 2030, Japan's market emphasizes technology adoption and continuous improvement, aligning with our value proposition.
  • South Korea: The Renewable Portfolio Standard (RPS) scheme and the Building Energy Efficiency Rating Certification System create growing demand for energy-saving technologies.

II. The Expansion Strategy: A Phased Approach to Asian Market Leadership

With a proven product and validated market demand, we are poised to execute an ambitious, phased expansion into Asia, targeting the largest and fastest-growing markets for our technology. This strategy leverages existing partnerships while building scalable operations to support rapid growth.

Phase 1: China Market Expansion (Months 1-12)

The initial phase focuses on leveraging our existing relationship with Chinese business partner Phoebe Wong and client success with Dongguan Zhuohaoyang PKG Co., Ltd to establish a scalable operation. Key actions include formalizing partnerships, developing China-specific marketing materials, initiating IP registration for our patent, and launching targeted campaigns in key industrial sectors like building materials, manufacturing, and petrochemicals.

Phase 2: Japan Market Entry (Months 13-24)

Our entry into Japan's sophisticated market will focus on patient relationship building and technical validation. We will engage local partners, pursue Japan-specific certifications, and execute pilot projects with leading industrial companies to build credibility and demonstrate our technology's effectiveness and non-invasive benefits.

Phase 3: South Korea Market Development (Months 25-36)

Entry into South Korea will build on lessons learned from China and Japan. The strategy will emphasize our technology's contribution to Green Standard for Energy and Environmental Design (G-SEED) certification requirements and its ability to enhance the global competitiveness of Korean industrial companies.
